## Reflections

### part one 

*First take:*
 - Part one introduces the expedition and sets us up to when the ship is finally beset and the floes break the hull forcing the men to abandon ship.  There was no hysteria, no chaos for the team as they had to leave the safety and warm quarters of the ship. 

- beginning of part one they introduce Shackleton and who he was a man and why he decided to take on the expedition.  It took years of work and fundraising and effort to get his imperial ex[pediction together. 

- just as they were to leave and finally head south the Great War began and was preceded by the assassinations and nation declaring war.  
  - Shackleton as his team and gained approval to postpone the expedition in favor of offering up there services to help win the war.  

- Fortitudine Vincimus: through and by endurance, we conquer is his family motto which he assumed for the rechristening of the ship.  

- first readings of this book and story i was drawn to the leadership required of shackleton to accomplish.  it was remarked because of not just the leadship to save everyone, but it was how the whole party remained together and didnt fall to despair or depression. 

later readings, im drawn to how many times the ‘plan’ and goal changed.  repeatedly it seemed everyday a new plan was decided.  and amid the confusion over the destination and chaos of the situation the men stood strong.  it is impressive because the men had good reason to break down and ‘lose’ it.  but they didnt.  i beleive partly because of leadship partly as they were a team and one didnt want to breakdown risking that of another.  

finally, i am also astonished at how the time was spent often in parties or celebrations.  the small things like holidays and birthdays remained even when it didnt seem like it was important when they could consider the existential crisis.  


"Thus their plight was naked and terrifying in its simplicity. Ifvthey were to get out—they had to get themselves out." (Page 8)

### shackleton impressions

```"whatever his mood - he had one pervading chaacaterisitic: he was purposeful." (Pg. 12)```

  - I can identify with him here and also in the following passage describing how he was 
'perennially entranced in new schemes' where he would surely finally win his fortune.  
  - he was also considered immature or lazy by some because of his need for adventure and extremes.  He couldn't seem to live a normal simple life.  He wanted more and needed more to give purpose behind his enormous capacity and fill the deep cavity of willpower he possessed. 
  - anything else that didnt move the whole being was not worth it. 

 ```"but in the antarctic - here was a burden which challenged eery atom of his strength" (p. 13)```


  ```"while Shackleton was undeniably out of place, even inept, in a great many everyday situations, he had a talent - genius, even - that he shared with only a handful of men throughout history - geniune leadership. He was, as one of his men put it, 'the greatest leader that ever came on God's earth, bar none'." (P. 13)```
  
	- **I would say I feel similarly.  I oscillate between a simple life in the woods and marching towards greatness by some venture.  The middle, the norm feels so boring and empty to me.  In the woods at least I not bothered by the mundane and even lame chasing of things and trinkets of today's world.  But that doesn't mean I am not materialistic. I love nice luxuries.  But if I find I can't afford luxuries I am perfectly content with what I have or nothing.  As long as I have my mind and my freedom and time to do the things I enjoy.** 

	- **the reason I don't give up and go to the woods is because inside of me I have a longing to know what I am made of.  I don't want to look back in years to wonder what if, what if I tried, what if I went away to Nashville and Austin.  Because I feel this fear of regret of NOT trying, it drives me into the world to pursue and chase and hunt and grow.**
